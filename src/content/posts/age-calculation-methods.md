How old are you? The answer depends on where you live and which cultural convention you follow.

## Western Age Counting

In most Western countries, you are zero years old at birth and gain a year on each birthday. This is the most common system and what our Age Calculator uses.

## East Asian Age Reckoning

In traditional Korean, Chinese, and Japanese systems, a baby is one year old at birth (counting time in the womb). Everyone gains a year on New Year Day, not their birthday. South Korea officially switched to the international system in 2023.

## Lunar Calendar Ages

Some cultures calculate age based on lunar calendars. The Chinese zodiac follows a 12-year cycle. Islamic calendar years are shorter (354 days), so ages calculated in Hijri years differ from Gregorian ages.

## Legal Age Definitions

Legal age calculations matter for voting, driving, drinking, and contracts. Most countries use completed years. Some use the calendar year of birth for school enrollment.
